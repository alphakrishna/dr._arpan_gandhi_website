Academic & Collaboration

Teaching, research, and collaboration have been woven into my clinical career from the very beginning. If you are a medical professional, researcher, institution, or organization looking to partner — this is where to start.

Mentorship

Shaping the Next Generation

Over the course of my career, I have mentored more than 200+ residents, fellows, and laboratory professionals — guiding them through the transition from trainee to independent clinical leader. This has been one of the most consistently rewarding aspects of my professional life.

Mentorship in diagnostic medicine is not simply about teaching technique. It is about helping young professionals develop clinical judgment, build confidence in uncertainty, learn how to communicate findings clearly, and understand how diagnostics fits into the broader picture of patient care.

  • Structured training programs for pathology residents and laboratory medicine fellows
  • Clinicopathological case discussions and diagnostic reasoning workshops
  • One-on-one mentoring for professionals navigating career transitions
  • Ocular pathology and oncology subspecialty guidance
  • Leadership development for laboratory professionals moving into management roles
Research & Innovation

Research & Clinical Innovation

My research interests span rare case documentation, diagnostic accuracy studies, quality systems in laboratory medicine, and the emerging intersection of AI and digital pathology. I am a published researcher, peer reviewer, and frequent contributor to clinical discussions on the future of diagnostics in India.

I am open to research collaborations, co-authorship, and advisory roles on projects that bridge clinical diagnostics with technology, public health, or healthcare systems innovation.

  • Rare and unusual case documentation and co-authorship
  • Diagnostic accuracy and quality benchmarking studies
  • AI-assisted pathology and digital diagnostics research
  • Genomics and molecular diagnostics integration in Indian healthcare
  • Peer review for pathology and laboratory medicine journals
Advisory

Board & Academic Advisory

I serve in advisory and board capacities for organizations where my experience in diagnostics, quality systems, and healthcare strategy can add genuine value — not as a title, but as an active contributor to governance, direction, and decision-making.

If you are building or reconstituting an academic advisory board, clinical advisory panel, or governance committee in the diagnostic or healthcare space, I welcome the conversation.

The most effective advisory relationships are built on honest dialogue, clear expectations, and a genuine alignment between the advisor's experience and the organization's real challenges — not just its stated ones.
